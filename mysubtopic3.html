Jonathan Loaisiga (Yankees #3)

Pictured: Jonathan Loaisiga (2018)

Jonathan Loaisiga is the third ranked Yankee prospect behind Justus Sheffield and Estevan Florial and is ranked 66th overall. Loaisiga was signed by the San Francisco Giants in 2012 but was released despite a strong pro debut because of injuries. The Yankees then signed him in February of 2016 after he threw 95 miles per hour during a tryout. That May, he made one Class-A start before needing Tommy John Surgery. He returned for 11 starts in 2017 and showed enough promise to be added to the Yankees 40-man roster in 2018. Seven months later, Loaisiga made his major league debut as a spot starter. In 14 AAA starts in 2018, he went 6-1 with a 2.89 earned run average with a minor-league-leading 8.38 strikeouts per walk. In 24.2 major league innings, Loaisiga struggled mightily but has the potential to become a good, middle of the rotation starter and even his floor leaves him with a bullpen role.
